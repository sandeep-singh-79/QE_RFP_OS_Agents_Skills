# Skill: Structuring & Consulting Thinking

## Purpose
Convert raw or fragmented inputs into prioritised, hierarchical, and leadership-readable structures that surface what matters most — so decision-makers can scan, comprehend, and act without needing to excavate the content.

Flat, unranked information forces the reader to do the prioritisation work. This skill does that work so the reader does not have to. That is what separates a consulting output from a data dump.

---

## Prerequisite Check — STOP If Conditions Are Not Met

Before structuring any content, perform this mandatory check:

1. **Is there substantive analysis or findings to structure?**
   - Check: Has QE Architect Thinking, Outcome & Risk Framing, or another analytical step produced findings?
   - If NO: State — *"Structuring & Consulting Thinking cannot run. There is no substantive analysis to structure. Run the relevant analytical skill first (e.g., QE Architect Thinking, Outcome & Risk Framing)."* Then HALT.

2. **Has Outcome & Risk Framing run (for risk-containing content)?**
   - Check: If the content contains risks, gaps, or findings — are they already classified by priority and urgency?
   - If NO: State — *"Structuring & Consulting Thinking cannot determine hierarchy without prioritized findings. Apply Outcome & Risk Framing first to establish what is High, Medium, and Low priority."* Then HALT.

**If either check fails, do not proceed.** This skill organizes and surfaces priority — it cannot create priority from unanalyzed content. Structuring without prior analysis produces false hierarchy.

## Four Core Structuring Rules — Apply All

### Rule 1: Hierarchy and Prioritization
Structure every output with clear levels and limited scope:
- **Primary message** — the single main takeaway
- **Secondary themes** — limit to 3–5 supporting areas maximum
- **Supporting detail** — explanation and evidence

Flat lists with no hierarchy fail. If more than 5 items feel equally important, challenge the framing — do not expand the list. Group or move lower-priority items to an appendix or notes layer.

### Rule 2: Insight-Led Communication
Lead with conclusions in both content and titles:
- **Headline bullets** state the insight or finding first
- **Sub-bullets** provide supporting rationale or evidence
- **Section titles** communicate the insight, not just the topic

Never bury the conclusion in a sub-bullet. Readers must understand the point before reading why.

Example section title — Prefer: *"Inconsistent coverage creates late-stage release exposure"* over *"Coverage gaps"*

### Rule 3: Length Discipline
Control volume to force clarity:
- Maximum 3–5 bullets per section
- Maximum 1–2 lines per bullet
- No paragraphs in primary sections

If content cannot fit within these limits, it belongs in a lower layer. Brevity is not optional — it is how you signal prioritization.

### Rule 4: Eliminate Duplication
Merge overlapping points rigorously. The same idea must not appear across sections under different wording — duplication signals unclear thinking and wastes the reader's time. One insight, one location.

## Choose a Deliberate Narrative Flow

Select the flow pattern that fits the content type — do not mix flows within a single document:

| Flow | Use When |
|------|----------|
| **Current → Gap → Impact** | Discovery readouts, gap analysis, problem framing |
| **Observation → Outcome → Decision** | Risk briefings, escalation documents, sponsor updates |
| **Now → Next → Later** | Roadmaps, phased plans, transformation timelines |

State the flow intention at the start of the structuring pass so it remains consistent throughout.

### How to Choose the Right Flow

Apply these selection criteria based on the primary goal of your content:

- **Current → Gap → Impact:** Use when the audience needs to understand what's missing or broken before discussing solutions or next steps. Emphasizes problem definition and consequence. Best for discovery findings, gap analyses, and maturity assessments.

- **Observation → Outcome → Decision:** Use when specific findings require immediate stakeholder action, approval, or decision. Emphasizes implication and urgency. Best for risk escalations, readiness assessments, and go/no-go recommendations.

- **Now → Next → Later:** Use when showing progression, sequencing, or phased implementation over time. Emphasizes timeline and ordering. Best for roadmaps, transformation journeys, and adoption plans.

**Rule of thumb:** If content focuses on problems, use Current/Gap/Impact. If content focuses on decisions, use Observation/Outcome/Decision. If content focuses on sequencing over time, use Now/Next/Later.

---

## Parking Rule
Any information that does not support the primary message must be moved — to an appendix, captured as supporting notes, or deferred. Completeness must not override clarity.

---

## Output Expectations

Produce:
- Clear three-level hierarchy throughout
- Controlled length within defined limits
- Scan-friendly layout with insight-led section titles
- One consistent narrative flow applied across the full output

---

## Do Not

- Generate new ideas or insights — structure what exists
- Assess risk severity or priority — that is Outcome & Risk Framing
- Perform domain translation — that is Domain & Context Adaptation
- Propose solutions or recommendations
